This part has 40 rounds. In each round, you will participate in an auction. At the beginning of every auction, you will be randomly matched with three other participants from a previous experiment.

What is the prize?

In every round a prize will be auctioned off. The prize will be a lottery ticket. Any lottery is characterized by two features:

  1. A Selected Value (in credits ): a non-zero amount of credits that the lottery possibly pays off and,
  2. A probability (in %): the probability of receiving the non-zero amount of credits.

For instance, consider the following lottery ticket:

With a probability of 70% you get 20 credits,
otherwise (with the complementary probability of 30%) you get 0 credits.

In this case, 20 credits is the Selected Value and 70% is the probability. This means that this lottery pays 20 credits with a 70% chance and pays 0 credits with a 30% chance. The outcome of this lottery is then either 20 credits or 0 credits.
