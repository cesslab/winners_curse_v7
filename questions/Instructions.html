The second Part II has also 40 rounds. Like in Part I, in each round you will see again lotteries with an unknown Selected Value, about which you will receive a signal. Remember that the Selected Value is no more than x numbers away from your signal.

Again, you will be randomly matched with three other participants from previous experiments. Remember that independently from each other the other three bidders also have received a signal surrounding the same Selected Value. The four of you might therefore receive different signals. That means, the four of you face the same lottery ticket, but the signal-updated range for the Selected Value may be different.

In every round you will have to answer three different questions. There is a right answer to each question. The closer your answer is to the truth, the more you will earn. For each question you may earn a maximum of 12 credits.

In this part you will not learn anything at the of the round. At the end of the experiment we will randomly choose one question for payment and only then you will learn how close your answer was to the correct answer for that particular question.
